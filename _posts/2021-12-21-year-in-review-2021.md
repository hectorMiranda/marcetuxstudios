2021 is the year I stopped drifting toward Rust and actually committed. The
language is real now — not a curiosity or a side project language but the thing
I'm going to be building with. The ownership model is so far from how I'd spent
the previous ten years writing code that it took the better part of six months
to stop fighting it and start trusting it. Once I stopped, the productivity came
quickly. The borrow checker is a collaborator, not an adversary.

The year at the bank was good work. The pipeline security improvements after
SolarWinds, the APIM policy cleanup, the distributed tracing, the .NET 5-to-6
migration, the Minimal APIs service — a solid year of solid work with a team I
respected. Log4Shell at the end was a reminder that the supply-chain problem I
wrote about in January is not solved and may not be solvable by any one team's
diligence; it requires industry infrastructure that doesn't fully exist yet.

The home bench is the quiet constant: LoRa mesh stable and collecting data, the
Commodore running, the Pi Zero gateway board in fab. What changes in January is
everything else. New problem domain, new language as the primary tool, new kind
of organization. The engineer who wrote that first post about OWIN in 2013 was
trying to understand a new middleware pipeline. The engineer writing this one is
trying to understand consensus and correctness in distributed systems with real
economic stakes. The learning is still the job. That hasn't changed at all.
