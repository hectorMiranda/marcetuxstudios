Two companies, one move, and a completely different stack in the second half — 2015 was
the year of deliberate change rather than gradual drift. At Spark Networks: closed out
the performance work, the service object refactoring, the SQS queue decoupling. Left
the codebase better than I found it, which I'll keep as my standard. At JibJab: FFMPEG
filter graphs, CloudFront at real scale, React alongside Ember, Apple IAP, holiday
traffic that hit and didn't break things. The observability investment in October paid
dividends in December in a way that justified every hour spent on it.

The language story this year: ES2015 finalized and Babel made it production-safe from
January; React's component model is the right way to think about UI at the scale I'm
working at; Swift open-sourcing is the signal that iOS development has a clear long-term
language. The infrastructure story: Let's Encrypt making HTTPS free and automatic is
the most important infrastructure change of the year, even if most of the impact is
still ahead of us.

The home network is running on ten months of unattended Pi 2 uptime: Mosquitto, InfluxDB,
Grafana, Jenkins, and now Let's Encrypt HTTPS on all of them. The ESP8266 nodes are
publishing sensor data at QoS 1 and the gaps in the history are gone. Culver City has
a proper workbench now. The move was right. 2016 will have more JibJab video pipeline
work, and I want to dig into Kubernetes before it becomes inevitable at work.
