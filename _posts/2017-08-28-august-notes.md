August had two threads that don't usually coexist: technical milestone and personal
transition. The .NET Core 2.0 release is the kind of milestone that closes a chapter —
the porting arguments are over, the ecosystem has arrived, the "but it doesn't have X"
objections are nearly gone. I've been waiting for this since 2014 and it's satisfying
to see it land fully.

The Go RN announcement is the other thing. The last two weeks at SolidCommerce I've
been documenting the systems I know best, writing runbooks, making sure the RabbitMQ
configuration and the Kubernetes manifests and the channel API quirks are in a form
someone can work from without me. Documentation written for a successor is the best
kind, because the author has skin in it being correct.

September will be a strange split month: finishing strong at SolidCommerce, starting
to think like a CTO for a healthcare staffing app. The Cordova mobile app is in beta
with a handful of healthcare facilities. The backend is scaling. The first engineering
hire conversation is one I'll be leading rather than participating in. That last one
is new territory and I expect to write about it.
