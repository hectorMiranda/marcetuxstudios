I gave notice at the bank last week. Three years as a solutions architect on the
Azure integration platform — good team, real work, and I learned a lot about how
large financial institutions make decisions about technology at the boundary between
"we control this" and "we don't." That's knowledge I'll carry forward. But I've
been watching the intersection of Rust and programmable distributed ledgers for
six months now, and the engineering problems there are the kind I want to work on.

The decision isn't "blockchain is going to replace banking" — I don't think that
and the claim is mostly noise. The decision is more specific: there's a serious
distributed systems and low-level systems engineering problem in writing correct,
auditable, high-performance code for a deterministic execution environment with
real economic stakes. That problem is interesting to me in the same way the CDN
work at Edgecast was interesting — not because of the business model but because
of the engineering constraints.

I'm taking four to six weeks before the next role starts. Some of that is Rust
deepening — I want to write real code in a language I've been studying, not just
exercises. Some of it is studying the consensus literature I've been circling.
The rest is probably finishing the ESP32+LoRa mesh and the Commodore restoration
and the two Altium boards that are three-quarters done. The next post after this
one will be from a different vantage point. That's the point.
