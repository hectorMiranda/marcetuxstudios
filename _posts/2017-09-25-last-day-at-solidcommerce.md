Today was the last day at SolidCommerce. Two years of marketplace integrations, message
queues, and the infrastructure work that underpins most of e-commerce without anyone
noticing it's there. I came in knowing .NET and AngularJS; I'm leaving knowing
RabbitMQ, Kubernetes, AWS at a deeper level than I had before, and a fair amount about
what it takes to keep a multi-channel e-commerce platform moving without dropping orders.

The thing I'll carry forward: the value of invisible work. The RabbitMQ pipeline doesn't
appear in a product demo. The Kubernetes namespace reorganization doesn't ship a feature.
The runbooks don't do anything until something breaks. But the morning when the Walmart
feed processor is backed up and the engineer on call solves it in ten minutes using the
runbook is the payoff, and it's a payoff that doesn't go in anyone's standup update.
Infrastructure work pays forward to people you'll never meet.

Go RN full-time starts Monday. The engineering team is two people, there are real users
in production, and I'm the person accountable for whether the technical choices we make
this year are the ones we'll be grateful for in two years. Different kind of weight than
"merge this PR when the tests are green." Looking forward to writing about it from the
inside.
