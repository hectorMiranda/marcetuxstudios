March was the month things started fitting together on the home side. InfluxDB plus
Grafana plus Mosquitto is the stack I was sketching in January, and it's running on
a shelf Pi that I have not had to touch since the initial setup. Three sensor nodes
publishing steadily, door state visible from the couch. The compounding investment is
real: each piece I understood in isolation is now part of a system that earns its keep
every day.

Work was mostly depth work: RSpec shared examples are cleaning up the service object
test suite, the Docker compose networking story is now coherent, and profile photo
uploads bypassing the Rails server was the kind of change that's been on the list for
two months. S3 presigned URLs are the kind of solution that makes you wonder what you
were doing before — it's obviously right, you just had to decide to do it.

React Native shipping for iOS is the most interesting thing that happened in front-end
land this month. Watching carefully, not shipping yet. N1QL from Couchbase is also
watching-carefully territory — the developer experience is right, the stability isn't
there yet. Some things you have to let bake.
