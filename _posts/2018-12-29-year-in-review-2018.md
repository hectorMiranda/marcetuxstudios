Three jobs in twelve months is not a career strategy I'd recommend. The Go RN exit
was planned; the CTM stint was the correction course that got me to the right role
faster than a longer wait would have. I'm at City National Bank as a solutions
architect, which is the role I was actually looking for, and the work is harder and
more interesting than anything I've done before. The year was expensive in transition
costs and valuable in calibration.

Technically, 2018 accelerated things I'd been watching: Kubernetes went from something
I had to convince teams to try to something I have to explain why you wouldn't use.
.NET Core 2.1 LTS gave the enterprise the stable target it needed to commit to the
migration. Azure is a real cloud platform with real managed services — App Services,
AKS, Service Bus, Key Vault — and the integration story is mature enough to build on.
The enterprise architecture patterns — API governance, ADRs, idempotency, distributed
tracing — are not new ideas, but applying them at a bank calibrated them in ways that
a startup context doesn't.

Going into 2019: the API modernization program at the bank is in a productive state.
I want to go deeper on observability — Application Insights is a start, but I want
the tracing picture to be complete before we add services. The Istio 1.0 evaluation
is in January. On the bench: another KiCad design is queued, and I want to get the
drone back in the air before spring. The blog got light in September and October as I
was orienting; January is back to regular.
