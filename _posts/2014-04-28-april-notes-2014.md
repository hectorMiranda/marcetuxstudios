April was Heartbleed first and everything else second. The incident burned a week, which
sounds like a lot until you compare it to what it would have cost without the process
skeleton we had. The runbook additions from the post-mortem are already incorporated. I'd
rather learn incident process on a well-contained vulnerability than on something that
caused actual data loss.

On the technology side, React is now on my "watch closely" list. The virtual DOM model
is genuinely different from what I've been building with, and I want to understand it
well enough to know when it's the right tool, not just an alternative. The Angular
portal stays Angular — consistency has value — but I'm not going to pretend the SPA
landscape isn't changing.

The job-looking is background noise for now. I talked to two companies, had one first
conversation that went nowhere interesting, and one that was more promising. Both are
in the online/consumer space which would be a different domain than CDN infrastructure.
The switch sounds appealing from a distance. Ask me again in June.
