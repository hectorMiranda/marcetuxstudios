The announcement dropped in late December and now it's January and I'm still not sure
what it means. Verizon acquires EdgeCast Networks. The CDN I've been building portals
for becomes a piece of a telco giant, and everyone in the office has been spending the
holidays reading between the lines of every official statement that says "nothing
changes for customers."

The truth is probably that nothing changes for a while, and then things change a lot.
Large acquisitions have a momentum of their own — integration teams, overlapping
tooling decisions, org structure shifts. The portals team sits in an interesting
position because we're product-adjacent: the UI layer everyone touches, close enough
to the business to feel whatever direction comes down. My guess is we have six months
of business-as-usual before any real turbulence.

What I can control is the code. The Q4 push left some rough edges — a couple of
Angular controllers that grew too fast and a caching layer I've been threatening to
refactor since October. January is cleaning that up and making the front end solid
before whatever comes next. Uncertainty is a good reason to tend the things you can
actually improve.
