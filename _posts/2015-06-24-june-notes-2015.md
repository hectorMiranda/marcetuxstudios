June was the month ES2015 stopped being a bet and became a standard, and also the month
I moved to Culver City and started having serious conversations about what's next. The
two things are not unrelated — the work I've been doing on ES2015 modules and Webpack on
evenings and weekends is the kind of thing that made those conversations possible.

The Let's Encrypt beta is the most infrastructure-significant thing to happen this month
even if I can't use it in production yet. Free, automated, ACME-protocol certificates
change the cost structure of running HTTPS everywhere from "requires effort and money"
to "requires a cron job." That's a real change.

The move happened, the bench is set up, the Pi is on the workbench where it belongs.
The ESP8266 nodes need to be repositioned — two of them were relying on the Santa Monica
apartment's topology — but the broker and InfluxDB came up clean after the move and the
data history survived. That's a good sign for the robustness of the setup.
