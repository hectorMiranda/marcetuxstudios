I've been at the bank for almost three years and the work is solid, the team is
good, and I'm bored in a specific way that I've learned to recognize. Not burned
out, not frustrated — just past the growth edge. The architect role here is well-
defined, the problems are well-understood, and I'm delivering inside a comfortable
envelope. Comfortable is not the same as satisfied, and I've been ignoring the
difference for about six months.

The Rust work and the Solidity reading are the leading indicator. When I'm doing
unpaid self-education in a technology that has nothing to do with the day job, I'm
usually building an exit ladder. I've done this before — the JibJab move in 2015
started with me reading FFMPEG documentation for fun — and I'm honest enough with
myself to name it. I want to build things in this space before the interesting
period is over. The interesting period in any technology is finite.

I'm not quitting tomorrow. The responsible version of this is: build enough
competence to be useful, talk to people already doing the work, understand the
economics. But I'm writing it down here because naming the thing is the first
step to doing something about it. The day job gets my full attention until I
leave. The evenings are for what comes next.
