February was the going-deeper month I promised myself, and it mostly delivered. The
front end got serious: Angular calls live behind services now, TypeScript is typing
that service layer and catching name bugs at compile time, source maps make the
minified bundle debuggable, and Bootstrap means the new tool looks like a tool instead
of a ransom note. The pieces are starting to feel like a *stack* rather than a pile.

The back end moved too. I finally understand where OWIN is heading and why the
middleware pipeline is the right model; SignalR 1.0 let me delete the dashboard's
polling timer for real push; and I made myself read an execution plan instead of
sprinkling indexes by feel. A theme keeps repeating from last year — do the work where
it belongs, then listen to the tool instead of guessing at it.

And the board is on a slow boat from a fab, which is the most nervous I've been about
something I can't change. March: the boards arrive and I find out how many footprints
I got backwards, the new tool ships behind its flags, and I want to take the OWIN
middleware idea past the toy stage into something real.
