August was the first full month at JibJab and it's been a steep and satisfying ramp.
FFMPEG filter graphs are now something I can write and debug rather than dread. SQS
queue-depth-driven scaling for the render workers is elegant in the same way the Spark
version was — the queue is the contract, the workers are stateless, the scaling policy
is the only configuration. It's the same pattern with a much bigger payload.

Ember's nested routing model clicked in the second week. The "routes fetch data,
components display it" separation is stricter than I'd have chosen intuitively, but
after seeing what it produces — small, testable components with no data-fetching
concerns — I'm sold. The opinionation is the point.

CloudFront in front of S3 is not a new idea but the JibJab scale makes the design
decisions concrete in a way they weren't when I was thinking about it abstractly. Cache
hit ratios, OAI policy, TTL calibration against real access logs — the same concepts
from the Edgecast days but now I'm operating them rather than selling them. Prefer the
view from the engine room.
