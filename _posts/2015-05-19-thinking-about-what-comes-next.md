About a year and a half at Spark Networks and I've been thinking about what the right
next move looks like. The stack here — Ruby on Rails, Couchbase, AWS, a mobile web
presence that's always one sprint away from a real app — is a good set of problems to
have worked through. The matching algorithm work, the queue decoupling, the performance
work this month, it's all solid. But the domain itself — online dating infrastructure —
has a ceiling I'm starting to see from here.

What I want to work on next is something with more interesting media or content
problems. Video, image processing, something where the data itself is complex and the
infrastructure to handle it is legitimately hard. Consumer-product companies rather than
enterprise. The ES6 and React Native work I've been doing on nights and weekends has
been the most interesting engineering I've done all year, and that's probably a signal.

I'm not in a rush — the role is stable and the team is good. But I've started doing
homework on a few companies in the LA area doing interesting work with video and media.
Culver City seems like the right geography given where I'm living now. Putting
intentions in writing tends to help me act on them.
