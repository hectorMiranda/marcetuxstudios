Twelve months ago I wrote that I was watching the LLM space with interest, unsure
what it meant for my career. I know what it means now. The year started with a job in
blockchain and ended with that job gone and a portfolio of LLM projects, a consulting
engagement doing retrieval work, and a clear sense of where I'm pointing next. That's
a faster pivot than I planned and a less disruptive one than I feared.

The technical arc is the one I'm proud of: from "I used ChatGPT and it was interesting"
in January to building end-to-end RAG pipelines, benchmarking embedding models,
running local inference on the home lab, understanding the function-calling API well
enough to build structured output pipelines, and writing down the operational lessons
that the tutorials skip. That's a real year of work. The evening-and-weekend time
investment was real. The CasperLabs job funded it.

Blockchain taught me Rust, and Rust is a gift that doesn't expire with the employer.
The language changes how I think about ownership, lifetimes, and concurrency in a way
that makes me better in every other language I touch. That was not the plan going in
but it's the outcome. 2024 looks like: full-time somewhere in the LLM/AI space, more
local inference experiments, and finding out how much of what I learned this year
survives contact with a production system at scale.

*Update: worth adding what I didn't have room for in the retrospective — the Mistral 7B release in September is the punctuation mark that closes the year's open-source story. In January the open-source option was "leaked weights with a legal question mark." In December it's a commercially licensed 7B model that runs on a Pi 5 and outperforms models twice its size. That's a year of progress compressed into one sentence.*
