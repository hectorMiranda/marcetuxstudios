Ten months in and the shape of the year is visible now. The consulting cadence found
a rhythm: two active clients at a time, project-based, usually two to four months
each. The tutoring grew to seven students, which is more than I can sustainably add
to. The technical focus narrowed around AI tooling infrastructure — retrieval,
evaluation, production guardrails — in a way that feels like a genuine specialization
rather than dilettantism.

The studio search is active. I looked at a ground-floor unit in Lincoln Heights twice
and the landlord is negotiable on the lease term. It's the right size, the right
light, and it's close enough to where I live that the commute wouldn't eat the benefit
of having the space. I haven't signed anything. I'm close to it.

.NET 9 ships in November, which I want to write about from a position of actually
having run the previews. The AI tooling landscape is moving fast enough that some of
what I wrote in January is already the established baseline — RAG with a reranker is
now table stakes, not an advanced technique. That's a good sign; it means the
practices are real, not blog-talk. The second half of the year has been more
consolidation than exploration. November and December will probably push the other
direction.
