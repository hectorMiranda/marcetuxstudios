November was infrastructure and people in roughly equal measure, which is an unusual
combination for a month. The CI/CD pipeline, the Terraform conversion, the production
hardening checklist — these are the things that make December's contract renewal
conversation go smoothly. Infrastructure work is usually invisible; this month it's
directly tied to a business outcome, which makes it easier to justify the time.

The hiring reflection is the thing I keep thinking about. The screening question about
a wrong decision is the most informative thing I've added to the process, and I don't
fully understand why it works so well. I think it's because it asks about something
that actually happened rather than something hypothetical, and the specificity of the
answer reveals whether the person has actually reflected on their own work or is
narrating a general principle they read about. Both are useful to know.

December is the pilot-to-contract month and I want to write about what it feels like
to deliver a product you built to users who are depending on it for their livelihood.
Healthcare shift workers are not a forgiving user base for reliability failures —
missing a shift notification isn't a minor inconvenience. That weight is appropriate
and I want to stay close to it.
