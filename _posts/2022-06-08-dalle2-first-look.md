OpenAI opened up the DALL-E 2 waitlist in April and I've been using it enough this month
to have a considered opinion. My prior for AI image generation was the GAN outputs I'd
seen in research papers — faces that were almost right, the occasional nightmare hand,
textures that fall apart under scrutiny. DALL-E 2 is a different kind of thing. It
generates photorealistic images from text prompts that I genuinely could not have
produced in Photoshop in any reasonable time, and the quality of the outputs still
catches me off guard two months in.

The prompt engineering is its own skill. "A 35mm photograph of a robot soldering a
PCB, depth of field, studio lighting" produces something wildly different from "a robot
soldering a PCB." The model responds to photographic vocabulary, art direction terms,
composition cues, artist name references. There's a lot of rapid iteration between what
you asked for and what you meant — which is, on reflection, the same challenge as
writing a precise specification for anything. The model is doing what you said, not
what you meant.

The part that's harder to sit with is the pace of it. Six months ago I would have said
that compelling visual image creation required either artistic training or professional
tools and years of practice. Now it requires a well-crafted sentence and a few seconds.
I'm an engineer, not a visual artist, and the image gen tools mostly make me more
productive. But I notice the assumption being invalidated, and I don't think I should
ignore that.
