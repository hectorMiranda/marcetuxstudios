April felt like the month things stopped being new and started being normal. I can write
a Casper contract entry point without consulting the docs for the API shapes. The
iterator combinators are my first instinct now, not an alternative I switch to
deliberately. The CEP-18 work gave me a real case study for how a well-understood
pattern (fungible tokens) maps to a different execution model, and the mapping is
informative even when it isn't one-to-one.

The dApp frontend work is the part I find most interesting from an engineering standpoint,
because the trust model is genuinely different. The app is not the authority; the chain
is the authority; the app is a lens. Building a good lens for a thing you don't control
is an interesting design constraint, and it pushes toward much more defensive state
management than a normal web frontend requires.

The LoRa mesh now has three nodes and actual routing. Small infrastructure that actually
works beats ambitious infrastructure that needs babysitting. The garage data shows up
in Grafana without me thinking about it, which is exactly where I wanted to get.
