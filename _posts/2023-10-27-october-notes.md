October was a transition month in the literal sense: the last weeks at one job, the
first weeks seriously interviewing for the next. I functioned as both a departing
employee and a job candidate simultaneously, which is not a comfortable mode but it's
a real one.

The LlamaIndex vs LangChain comparison finally landed after months of running both.
The short version is that they're solving different parts of the problem and the
frustration people have with each usually comes from using the wrong one for the job.
I'll update that post when I have more experience with what the combination looks like
at a longer time scale.

The multi-step reasoning post is the one I keep getting asked about in interviews,
which is gratifying and a little ironic — I wrote it to organize my thoughts for
those conversations and it turned out to be useful as a reference in those conversations.
November: something resolves, one way or another.
