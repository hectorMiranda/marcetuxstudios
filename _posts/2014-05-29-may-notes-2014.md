May was a month in two registers: the day job at EdgeCast, where the backplane work
landed cleanly in production and the load test confirmed it, and the job search, which
has reached the final-round stage with one company and is still live with two others.
The Couchbase reading paid off — the technical screen at Spark went well, and I walked
out of the last interview not entirely sure what I'd said but thinking it had gone okay.

The move to Santa Monica is done. The boxes are mostly unpacked, the Printrbot is
running on the kitchen table, and the commute to EdgeCast is now a different kind of
bad — beach traffic instead of freeway traffic, which at least moves. I'll probably
switch to a bike or the bus within a month.

Technically: the Gulp build is now the fastest it's been, the SignalR scale story is
solid, and I cleaned up a class of HTTP mocking in the test suite that's been bothering
me since Q1. If the job offer comes through in June I'll be leaving on a high note,
which is the right way to leave.
