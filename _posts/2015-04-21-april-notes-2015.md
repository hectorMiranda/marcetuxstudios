April closed a few things that had been in the "figure this out" column. Sidekiq moved
email out of the request cycle and the P99 tail dropped noticeably — the kind of change
that's obviously right once you do it and mystifying that you waited. CloudWatch alarms
for the SQS queues should have existed in January; they exist now. The `EXPLAIN ANALYZE`
habit is still something I have to make myself do instead of reaching for indexes first,
but I'm running it more consistently.

The home side: OTA firmware updates on the bathroom node mean I no longer need to
physically pull the thing to flash it. The non-blocking loop pattern that OTA requires
is also just better code — sensors that don't block `loop()` are sensors that respond
to OTA transfers, and also sensors that don't stall on a slow WiFi reconnect. Good
constraints produce good code.

Rails concerns: the verdict is that they're useful for exactly one thing (shared
contracts across models) and reach for too readily for another (making fat models
into distributed fat models). Service objects are still the right tool for the latter.
That's probably the cleanest design principle I've articulated this month, and it took
three months of using concerns to see it clearly.
