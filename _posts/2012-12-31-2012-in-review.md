Four months of writing things down, and the experiment has earned a permanent
place. The blog is now the first place I look when I half-remember solving
something, and that alone has paid for the time.

What 2012 was about, in one breath: the web got **real-time** (SignalR, WebSockets)
and **responsive** (Bootstrap, the still-unsolved image problem); .NET got
**async** and started decoupling from IIS (OWIN on the horizon); shipping got
**boring on purpose** (Git, Jenkins, Octopus, toward continuous deployment); and the
bench taught me the same lessons the day job did — cache the reads, keep state where
it belongs, calibrate against the environment, survive a power cut.

The `examples/` folder turned out to be the part I'm proudest of — talk is cheap,
runnable code isn't. A dozen small things you can actually clone and try.

2013 plans: go deeper on the single-page-app shift that's clearly coming, get
serious about test coverage, and finally design a PCB instead of living on
breadboards. Happy new year. — marcetux
