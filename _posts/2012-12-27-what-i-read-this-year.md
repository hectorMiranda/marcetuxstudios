Between Christmas and New Year's, the annual look at what actually stuck from a
year of reading. I'm bad at finishing programming books — most are reference you
raid, not narratives you read — but a few earned their place this year.

*Release It!* reframed how I think about production. It's not about features; it's
about what happens at 3am — timeouts, bulkheads, circuit breakers, the failure modes
that only show up under load. Half of this year's resilience posts trace back to it.
*The Pragmatic Programmer* I reread; it ages better than almost anything. And I
finally worked through enough of *CLR via C#* to actually understand what `async`
compiles down to instead of treating it as magic.

The non-book lesson: the highest-leverage reading this year was other people's
**post-mortems and source code**, not books at all. Reading how a system failed,
written honestly, teaches faster than any chapter on best practices.
