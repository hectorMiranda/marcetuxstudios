The formal job search started Monday. I updated the resume to lead with the LLM and
retrieval work — the RAG pipelines, the pgvector experiments, the Llama 2 local
inference setup — even though all of it was evenings and weekends. The Rust at
CasperLabs is the headline of the last year; the AI work is the direction I'm pointing.
Making that clear on paper means deciding what story I'm telling, and the story is
"this is the thing I've been building toward."

The market for "AI engineer" in late 2023 is unusual. There's a lot of demand and the
signal-to-noise in job postings is terrible — many companies are copying phrases from
TechCrunch articles rather than describing actual work. I'm filtering for companies
that mention specific technical choices (RAG, vector stores, eval frameworks, inference
latency) rather than generic "AI-powered" language. A posting that says "build
retrieval pipelines with pgvector and evaluate with RAGAS" is a different kind of job
than one that says "leverage cutting-edge AI."

The Rust background is a useful differentiator in unexpected ways. Several companies
doing local inference or edge AI care a lot about Rust, and blockchain work, for all
its product-level disappointments, produced genuinely rigorous Rust. A year of writing
production Rust under correctness constraints is different from a year of toy projects
in Rust, and that shows in code.
