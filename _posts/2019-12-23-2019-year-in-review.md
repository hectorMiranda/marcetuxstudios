The year that felt like building things that will matter for a while. The pipeline infrastructure, the observability stack, the service templates — all of them compound. Every new service the bank stands up now starts from a foundation that took most of the year to assemble. In January I was proving that YAML pipelines were better than the visual designer; in December I'm saying "use the template" and new teams get the whole year's worth of decisions for free.

The technology arc had a clear shape: .NET Core 3.0 and 3.1 validated that the platform is genuinely where enterprise .NET belongs now. Blazor Server is interesting but not for our primary use case. gRPC is production-proven for the right workloads. The observability investment paid off faster than I expected. Angular 8 and the Ivy preview position the frontend well for 2020.

Home side: the Pi 4 is the upgrade that made the home lab feel current again. The energy monitor v2 is the most technically satisfying hardware project I've finished — it required analog circuit design, PCB layout, firmware, a data pipeline, and a dashboard, all of it working together. Good reminder that the home projects are where I get to own the full stack. The bank work is rewarding in a different way — at scale, with constraints, with colleagues — but there's something irreplaceable about tracing a problem from a noisy ADC sample all the way back to a filter design decision and fixing it in silicon.
