The Verizon integration is moving into its real phase now — there are meetings about
branding alignment and tooling standardization that weren't happening in January.
Nothing catastrophic, just the organizational gravity of a large company beginning to
pull. And I've been honest with myself that the pull is part of what made me start
looking at the job boards this month for the first time in two years.

I'm not sure what I want to move toward, but I know what I want to stay in: product-close
engineering, web stack, small-to-medium teams where the technology decisions aren't made
three layers above you. The thing I don't want is the version of "senior developer" that
means attending meetings about API standards without actually building anything. I've seen
that path from the outside and it doesn't look like what I got into this for.

The conversations are exploratory right now. LA has a decent tech scene outside the usual
industry corridors, and a few companies I've been watching are hiring. Nothing concrete to
report, but the posture has shifted from passive to active. Taking notes on what I care
about before I'm in the middle of deciding, so the decision isn't just reactionary.
