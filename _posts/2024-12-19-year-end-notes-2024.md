Twelve months between full-time roles, which is the longest I've been without one
since U. of Veracruz. The math: eight consulting clients over the year, seven
tutoring students at the peak, one lease signed. The financial outcome is fine. The
professional outcome is better than fine — I'm more technically current on AI
infrastructure than I would have been in any full-time role I was offered in January.
The personal outcome is the best: there is now a space with my name on the door, and
the work that happens there has a home.

What I learned this year: the gap is a form of work. It doesn't feel like it from the
outside and some days it doesn't feel like it from the inside either, but staying
current, building systems no one is paying you to build, teaching other people —
these produce the kind of knowledge that doesn't fit in a job description. The tutoring
in particular changed what I think I know. I went in thinking I was sharing knowledge;
I came out having mapped the difference between using something and understanding it.

The heading into 2025: I have the studio, I have the clients, and the MCP protocol
is the thing I want to go deep on in Q1 before it's no longer an early-adopter
opportunity. The marcetuxstudios name that I wrote in my notes in March is on the
door in December. That's a better-than-expected outcome for a year I started by
being unemployed. Next year the question is what gets built in the space.
