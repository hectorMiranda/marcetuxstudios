The year split cleanly in two. January through September at SolidCommerce: marketplace
integrations at scale, the RabbitMQ pipeline, Kubernetes in production, Angular 4 and
5, the Node microservice experiment, the Webpack 3 scope hoisting win. Work I'm proud
of, mostly invisible to anyone outside the engineering org, which is the nature of
infrastructure. The order pipeline ran. The channels didn't drop feeds. The ops team
had a dashboard.

October through December at Go RN: a completely different kind of work. The code I
wrote was smaller in volume and larger in consequence — not because I'm writing more
important code, but because the team is small enough that my architectural choices are
the shared architecture. The first hire, the CI/CD pipeline, the Terraform conversion,
the production hardening, the GraphQL subscriptions, the shift-events audit table. All
of it builds toward a product that healthcare workers are using for their livelihood.

The thing I didn't expect about the CTO role: how much of it is being honest about
uncertainty. A senior engineer can say "I think X is the right call" and be respected
for the opinion. A CTO says the same thing to a CEO or a board and is expected to have
quantified the risk. That's a different muscle and I'm still building it. 2018 will be
the year I either get comfortable with it or find out that I'm better suited to
a different kind of role. I don't think it will be the latter, but I'm writing it down
because that's what intellectual honesty requires.
