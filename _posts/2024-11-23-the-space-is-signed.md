I signed the lease on a unit in Lincoln Heights this week. 200 N San Fernando Rd,
ground floor, about 450 square feet. Bare concrete, two windows facing north, a
loading door in the back. It's a former light-industrial space and it looks like one.
That's exactly what I wanted — nothing has been done to it that I'd have to undo.

The plan for December: get the bench set up, get the lab machines moved in, get the
Pi cluster off the kitchen shelf and onto a proper rack. The home lab being in an
actual space instead of scattered across a house is going to change how I work in
ways I can only partly predict. The predictable part: the friction of starting drops.
When the oscilloscope is already out, when Ollama is already running, when the
soldering iron is two feet away instead of in a cabinet, the sessions happen.

The name that's been living in my notes since March: marcetuxstudios. This is what
that means. Not a LLC, not a brand, not a business plan — just a place where the
work happens and a name for it. I'm going to write here about what gets built there.
The first thing to build is the bench itself, which I've been designing on paper for
three months and can now actually assemble.
