First post of the year, and the thing I most want to get right in 2013 is picking a
front-end direction. The dashboards have outgrown jQuery-plus-Knockout, and there
are now three serious contenders for "real app in the browser."

**Backbone** I already use — minimal, you assemble the app yourself, maximum control
and maximum boilerplate. **Ember** is the opposite: opinionated, convention-heavy,
does a lot for you if you build it Ember's way. **AngularJS** sits in between and is
the one making me curious — two-way binding like Knockout but a whole app framework
around it, with dependency injection and directives for building reusable
components.

I'm not deciding by reading; I'm going to build the same small screen in Angular
this month and see how it feels under my hands. Surveys and benchmarks lie; the
truth is in how fast you can change something three weeks later. More once I've
actually shipped something in it.
