Three months, three jobs. I'm done moving. The City National Bank role is the one I
was building toward, and the onboarding friction of the first two weeks has given way
to the enjoyable part: actual design conversations with application teams, actual
technical decisions that matter, and actual peers on the architecture team who've been
doing enterprise integration longer than I've been doing it seriously. The learning
curve is steeper than I expected and I'm glad it is.

The OpenAPI governance work is satisfying in a way I didn't predict. Writing lint rules
that encode institutional knowledge is a form of documentation that fights back —
the doc degrades gracefully because the tool still runs. The AAD auth patterns are the
deepest I've gone on OAuth flows since reading the RFC in 2015, and the managed identity
story is genuinely elegant once you understand the identity assignment model.

Home: the pulse-oximeter KiCad board is ordered. It's been a year since I first
started it, which is embarrassing, but the footprint is right and the design review
with a friend in Slack caught two decoupling capacitor placements that would have
caused grief at bring-up. The drone is on a shelf. September had no weekend time for
flight.
