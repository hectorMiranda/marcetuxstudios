The studio's bench area has three 20-amp circuits now, which is the number I landed on
after counting the oscilloscope, the soldering station, the hot-air rework station, the
bench power supplies, and the thought that I'd like to run all of them at once without
tripping a breaker. The electrician finished Thursday. I spent Saturday running cable
trays and labeling every outlet — the kind of thing that takes half a day and saves you
weeks over time.

The bench surface is an 8-foot butcher-block top mounted to adjustable steel legs, same
style I've seen in proper hardware labs. Height-adjustable means I can sit for close
PCB work and stand for anything involving the oscilloscope and probe clips at once.
Under the bench: a lockable drawer unit for tools, and a lower shelf where the power
supplies live permanently rather than getting pushed aside when the surface gets busy.

The Pi cluster and the NAS move in next week once I get the rack mounted. I'm keeping
the rack on a rolling cart so I can pull it out for cable management instead of doing
that work against a wall. The lesson from every previous workspace: whatever's hardest
to access will need the most attention. Make it roll.
