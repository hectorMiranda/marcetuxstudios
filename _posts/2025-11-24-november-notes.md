The mysterious 6W on the rack circuit turned out to be a USB hub I'd plugged in and
forgotten, still powering a device I thought was off. The power monitor found it in
an evening of looking at the per-circuit trend. That's a $40 board paying for itself
in recovered attention before the first month is out.

November is the month I let the year start to settle. The consulting pipeline is full,
the studio is operational and comfortable, and the hardware projects for the year are
done. The RF front-end, the CO2 sensors, the power monitor — three boards that moved
from "I should build that" to "running and in Grafana," which is three more than
average. The new space is the reason.

The multi-modal agent work is where the interesting consulting questions are going
right now. Teams that are past the text-only phase are asking about image and document
inputs, and the practical limitations are more nuanced than the marketing suggests.
The .NET 10 preview builds look good; I'll have a proper evaluation post when the
final release lands in spring.
