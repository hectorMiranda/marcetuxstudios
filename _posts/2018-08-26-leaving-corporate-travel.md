I accepted an offer at City National Bank and will start in September. Three and a
half months at CTM — a genuinely short stint and I'm not going to dress that up as
a planned exploration. The role was correctly described and the work was real, but
the company's enterprise architecture direction and my own career direction weren't
pointing the same way. I'd rather acknowledge that quickly and move on than wait
until both sides are frustrated.

What I took from it: Mulesoft and API-led connectivity are legitimate patterns worth
understanding deeply, not just vendor talking points. The data governance and GDPR
work calibrated my instincts on what enterprise compliance actually looks like in a
running system. And the Azure time — AKS, App Services, ARM templates — is directly
applicable to where City National Bank is heading with its modernization roadmap.

The bank architect role is the job I was actually looking for when I left Go RN. More
design work, more complex integration requirements, a team large enough to learn from
laterally. I'm going in with clearer expectations than either of the last two transitions.
That's the advantage of changing jobs badly — you get calibrated faster.
