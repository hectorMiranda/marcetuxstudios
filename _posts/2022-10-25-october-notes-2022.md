October had a satisfying arc: the closures bug at the start forced me to go back to
fundamentals, which is often where the most durable learning happens. The WASM component
model post is one I want to be able to look back at in a few years as a record of where
the technology was in 2022 — early, theoretical, pointing at the right problem. The
gRPC health check work is the kind of quiet infrastructure improvement that nobody
celebrates until the day the alert fires at 2am and the on-call engineer knows exactly
which node went down.

The Rust knowledge is past the "fighting the compiler" stage and into something I'd
call fluent. I catch borrow checker issues in my head before I write the code, which
means I write the correct code faster rather than writing and rewriting. That's what
fluency is: the automation of things that used to require deliberate attention.

November is going to be interesting. .NET 7 is supposed to ship, which I'll have
opinions about even though my day job is full Rust now. And the Casper contract work
is ramping toward a mainnet deployment, which changes the cost of a bug in ways that
focus the mind usefully.
