Jenkins catches a broken build *after* you push. A `pre-commit` hook catches it
before it leaves your machine, which is cheaper for everyone. I added a couple of
local hooks this week and the noise on the team dropped noticeably.

The hooks are just scripts in `.git/hooks`. My `pre-commit` does the boring,
high-value checks: refuse commits that contain a stray `Console.WriteLine` debug
line or a `TODO: remove`, and run a fast lint. Nothing slow — a pre-commit hook that
takes 30 seconds gets disabled by week two.

The catch worth knowing: hooks live in `.git/`, so they **don't** travel with a
clone. You can't force them on the team through the repo alone. We keep the scripts
in a tracked `hooks/` folder with a one-line install step in the README. Opt-in, but
easy to opt into.
