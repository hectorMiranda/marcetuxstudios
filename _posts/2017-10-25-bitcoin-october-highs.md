Bitcoin crossed $6,000 this week. In July I wrote about watching the bubble from the
side and I want to revisit that from the same position because the temperature in the
room has changed noticeably. The ICO market is now generating projects that I cannot
find a charitable technical interpretation for. The number of engineers who are
switching to blockchain projects for financial reasons rather than technical interest
is visible in the conversations I'm having.

The engineering things I still find interesting haven't changed: the consensus
mechanism, the cryptographic primitives, the UTXO model, the Lightning Network
specification as a solution to on-chain throughput limits. These are genuinely
interesting problems. The applications built on top of them that describe themselves
as "decentralized [noun]" without a clear reason why decentralization improves the
noun — that's the part I remain skeptical of.

My position unchanged from July: I'm not investing in ICOs. The engineering underneath
is worth understanding. The speculation on top is a different activity, and I don't
want to pretend I can evaluate it well enough to stake money on it. The thing I'm
watching more carefully than price: how the regulatory environment develops. The SEC's
Howey Test applied to tokens is going to resolve a lot of ambiguity in a direction
that the ICO market is not pricing in. That's my actual opinion, and I could be wrong
in either direction.
