WannaCry was the event of the month and it forced work that should have been happening
anyway. The patch compliance dashboard is live and showing numbers nobody was
comfortable with. That's the useful outcome. The HTTPS internal API work is slower —
internal services take longer to migrate than external ones because the integration
tests all need updating — but it's moving.

The feed error taxonomy is the quietly satisfying win. The support queue for
marketplace errors dropped noticeably in the two weeks since it went live, mostly
because sellers now get a message that tells them what to fix rather than an opaque
channel error code. The ops team wrote back to say it was the best thing we'd shipped
in months. I'll take it.

Home side: the garage sensor is on deep sleep, the battery is holding, and I have
exactly one bar of useful data about garage temperature that doesn't change much.
The door-state sensor is next — a magnetic reed switch on GPIO that wakes the chip
on change rather than on a timer. That should be more interesting than temperature in
a concrete garage.
