The CasperLabs chapter closed quietly in December and I've been sitting with that for
a few weeks. It's the first time in twelve years I've chosen the gap intentionally —
not a layoff, not a rushed pivot, but a deliberate pause to figure out what comes
next. The honest answer is I don't know yet, which is both unsettling and the most
interesting I've felt professionally in a while.

What I do know is that 2023 broke something loose in me around language models. I
came into last year skeptical — I'd seen hype cycles (web3 was still fresh) — and I
left it genuinely changed. GPT-4 in March was one of those moments where I had to
revise my priors. The embedding and RAG work I did in the second half of the year
felt like real engineering, not demo-ware. So January is going to be: consulting
leads, tutoring some CS students who reached out, and going deep on the parts of this
AI tooling stack I understand shallowly.

I'm also setting up a proper home lab bench again. The Pi 5 arrived late November,
Ollama is running, and I want to see how far a local model gets before I need to hit
an API. The gap is a luxury; I intend to use it.
