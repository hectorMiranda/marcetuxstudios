November is the month everything accelerated. .NET 6 shipped, I shipped a new
microservice on it the same week, C# 10 landed exactly the language features I'd
been waiting for, and I gave notice. There's a pleasing completeness to the timing —
ending three years of .NET work the same month .NET 6 landed, when the platform
is in genuinely good shape, feels like leaving a tidy desk rather than an unfinished
one.

The Rust traits post is the kind I'm going to be writing more of in the coming
months — not "here's what I did at work" but "here's what I understand now that I
didn't before." The learning is the job for a few weeks and I'm not going to
apologize for that. The trait/interface distinction took me two weeks to really
internalize and writing it out is how I know I've got it.

The bench is busy. The Commodore is fully restored and actually running some
BASIC programs I remembered from being eight years old. The LoRa mesh is stable.
The new Altium board — a LoRa gateway with a Pi Zero on it — is in layout. December
is going to be a read/build/study month with a headline event I'm not looking
forward to: Log4Shell is apparently the security news of the year, and I have two
former colleagues still dealing with it. More on that next month.
