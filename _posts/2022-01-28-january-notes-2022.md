January was an orientation month in the best sense — steep, useful, and mostly humbling.
The Rust borrow checker stopped feeling adversarial about halfway through the month,
which is probably the most important thing that happened. Once the ownership model
clicks it stops being a series of compiler errors to placate and starts being a set of
questions the language is asking about your design. Those are better questions than I
was asking myself in C# most of the time.

The Casper-specific knowledge is still accumulating. The deploy flow is mechanical now,
the global state trie makes sense as a data model, and I have a working local test loop.
What I don't have yet is intuition about contract design — when to reach for named
keys versus dictionaries, how to structure upgrades, what the gas model punishes. That's
what February is for.

At home, the LoRa mesh nodes are finally on proper sleep-wake cycles and running for
weeks on a charge instead of days. Small win, but the kind that means the network
actually works as ambient infrastructure rather than a thing I have to babysit. That's
the goal: boring at the boundary, as quiet as it can be while still doing the job.
