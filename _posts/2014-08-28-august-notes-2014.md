Three months at Spark and the new stack is starting to feel like a stack rather than a
list of unfamiliar tools. The S3 presigned upload change landed this week and the
Unicorn worker utilization graph shows it immediately — the workers are available during
what used to be peak photo-upload load. That kind of observable improvement is satisfying
in a way that purely correctness-focused changes aren't.

The SQS/worker architecture is making more sense to me now. The at-least-once delivery
guarantee is a design constraint that forces idempotency into the worker logic, which is
the right constraint. Systems that assume exactly-once delivery are fragile; systems that
handle duplicates gracefully are robust. It's a tax that pays dividends.

The ESP8266 standalone experiments are going well. It's sitting on a piece of breadboard
taped to the side of the kitchen cabinet posting temperature readings every minute. The
data goes into a small SQLite file on the laptop for now; I want to visualize it, which
means either a quick Rails API on localhost or something creative. Probably the Rails
API, because at this point Rails is faster than thinking about alternatives.
