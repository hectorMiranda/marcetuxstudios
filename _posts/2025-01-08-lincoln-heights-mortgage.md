Signed the papers on Thursday. 200 N San Fernando Rd in Lincoln Heights — a mixed-use
unit with enough square footage to finally pull the home lab and electronics bench out
of the bedroom and give them a real room. This is the thing I've been circling for two
years, and the "marcetuxstudios" idea I'd been running as a concept now has a physical
address.

The space is raw. Concrete floors, exposed conduit on one wall, a freight door that
needs a new motor. The previous tenant ran a small print shop here, which left behind
some genuinely useful shelving and an overhead lighting rig that's actually good for
bench work. The electrical panel was upgraded, which means I don't have to beg the
building to run 20-amp circuits for the test gear. That was the thing I was most
worried about.

Plan for the next several months: rewire the bench area, get the rack in, move the Pi
cluster and the NAS, set up the Altium workstation in a proper spot. I'll probably do
one room at a time rather than a big-bang move. The blog has a home now — the studio is
where the posts will get written.
