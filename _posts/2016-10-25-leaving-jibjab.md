I put in my notice this week. The end-of-year target is deliberate — I want to close
out the things I started, hand off the pipeline work to the team cleanly, and not leave
in the middle of a sprint. The year-end timing also gives me a natural pause before the
next thing, which I haven't fully decided on yet.

JibJab was a good run. A video platform is a different domain than the CDN work at
Edgecast or the Rails apps at Spark, and the pipeline problems — transcoding at scale,
CDN economics, queue-driven reliability patterns — are problems I'm glad I had because
they're the kind of thing you only really understand by breaking them in production.
The ESP8266 and KiCad work on the side fed the same interest from a different angle:
building things that work reliably in environments you can't fully control.

What I'm looking for next is something where the engineering problems are less well-
trodden. The marketplace/platform space has problems I haven't worked on. The .NET Core
port I did this year made me want to build something on it from scratch rather than
porting. And the Kubernetes conversations at JibJab that were always "we'll get there
eventually" will be the first decision on a fresh stack rather than the tenth. The plan
for November and December is: finish well here, don't rush the next decision.
