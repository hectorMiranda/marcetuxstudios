Three weeks without a job title. The consulting engagement is part-time and keeps the
hands busy, but it's not the full-time role I'm looking for and both sides know that.
This is the first time I've been between substantial roles in years, and the first week
was uncomfortable in the way that unstructured time is uncomfortable when you've been
structured for a long time.

By the third week it's more useful than uncomfortable. I've been reading papers —
actual papers, not blog summaries of papers — about retrieval architectures, attention
mechanisms, and evaluation methodology. That reading would have happened in 20-minute
increments on weekday mornings if I were employed full time. Instead it's happening in
three-hour blocks and the understanding is different. There's a lot you can learn
quickly when learning is the job.

The LLM space I'm pointing toward keeps generating interesting problems: how do you
evaluate a system whose outputs are natural language? How do you do this without
making model-judging-model calls that are expensive and circular? How do you design a
retrieval system for a corpus that changes faster than you can reindex it? I don't
have complete answers to any of these, but I know they're the interesting questions,
which is enough to know I'm pointing at the right field.
