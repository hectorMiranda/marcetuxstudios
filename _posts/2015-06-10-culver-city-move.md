Moved into the Culver City apartment this weekend — the Santa Monica lease was up and
Culver City is where I should have been the whole time. Better geography for the kinds
of companies I've been talking to, better neighborhood, and the apartment has an actual
spare room I can turn into a proper workbench space instead of the kitchen-table setup
I've been tolerating for a year and a half.

The bench setup is the first project: a proper six-foot workbench with a pegboard
behind it, the oscilloscope actually mounted where I can reach it, and the Pi 2 hub
relocated from the living room shelf to the bench where it belongs. The ESP8266 sensor
network is going to need a few nodes repositioned but that's an afternoon of work, not
a project.

The professional part: I've been in conversations with a company in Culver City that
does video and media content — which is exactly the domain I said I wanted in the May
notes. I'm not going to name it until there's something to name, but the interviews have
been interesting and the stack is genuinely different from what I've been doing. Video
pipelines, AWS at scale, consumer product. If it goes the way I'm hoping, the summer
will involve learning a lot of FFMPEG.
