The offer from Spark Networks came through in late May and the start date is June 16th.
This week is the last week at EdgeCast, and I'm spending part of it writing up the
systems I built so the next person doesn't start from zero. Documentation that you write
leaving a job is the most honest documentation you'll ever write — you have nothing to
defend and no incentive to make it look more complete than it is.

The two years here were worth it in ways I couldn't have predicted going in. Building
portals for a CDN means you think about HTTP at a level of detail you don't if your
apps just use it. The caching, the `Cache-Control` semantics, the edge-behavior
differences, the TLS operational realities — that knowledge is embedded now in a way that
classroom learning never achieves. Heartbleed accelerated the security education by
about two years of normal career growth.

The thing I'm leaving with that surprised me: I care a lot about the operational side.
Not DevOps as a job title, but the habit of thinking "what happens when this breaks at
2 AM" as a design constraint, not an afterthought. I want to bring that to the new job,
where the stack is completely different and the domain is consumer-facing. Taking the
mindset, leaving the .NET.
