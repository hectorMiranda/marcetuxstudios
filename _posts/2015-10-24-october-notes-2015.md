October was the month the render pipeline gained proper observability and the video
library got the React migration I'd been planning since July. SQS dead-letter queues
plus structured CloudWatch logging means the next render failure will be a debugging
exercise rather than a mystery. That's the infrastructure work that doesn't ship a
feature but makes features shippable without anxiety.

The Flux pattern is working at the library scale. The action-log debuggability paid off
immediately when a state bug in the sidebar turned out to be two actions firing in the
wrong order — visible in the log, fixable in five minutes. That's the kind of debugging
that takes two hours without the structure.

Home side: MQTT QoS 1 on the sensor nodes has eliminated the missed-reading gaps in
InfluxDB. The broker restarted for a kernel update last week and every reading from
every node came through cleanly on reconnect. Nine months of investment in that stack
and it's at the point where I don't think about it — which is the point.
