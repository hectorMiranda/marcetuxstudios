July was the best month of the year so far, mostly because of Sydney. The Dogeathon
reminded me that the best parts of this industry are the people who show up to build
something with limited resources and no guarantee it'll work. We came second. The team
that beat us built an on-chain poker game with working card shuffling logic in the
contract. They deserved to win.

The contract upgrade work this month finished something that started as a theoretical
understanding in February. I knew how upgrades were supposed to work from reading the
docs; I now know how they actually work from doing one while being careful about all
the places it could go wrong. That difference — between knowing the spec and having
internalized the failure modes — is most of what experience adds to a skill.

The Unity work is shelved for now; game dev was a two-day adventure and I'm back in
the Rust-and-blockchain world. But I might keep the tilemap notes around. There's a
corner of the home lab dashboard that could use better visualization, and "paint tiles
on a grid" is not a bad model for floor plans.
