First full month at Go RN and the pacing is different from anything I've had before.
Fewer deep-focus coding sessions, more context switching between product decisions and
technical ones. I'm learning to protect at least half a day of uninterrupted coding —
the CTO who doesn't write code becomes the CTO who can't read code in six months, and
I've seen that happen.

The first engineer I hired is ramping up well. The code review feedback is landing; the
architecture conversations are collaborative. The thing I'm still calibrating is how
much I want to explain the "why" versus letting them discover it. Too much explanation
is condescending; too little is setting someone up to repeat mistakes that cost time.
The shift-events audit table decision, for example — I could have handed them a schema.
Instead I described the requirement ("we need to prove what happened in any shift
dispute") and let the schema come from the discussion. The schema that came out was
better than the one I had in my head.

November has a product milestone: the pilot facilities we brought on in September go
to contract in December, which means the features they're depending on need to be
production-hardened, not just working. Different standard. Getting there.
