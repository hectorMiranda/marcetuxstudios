The apartment in Diamond Bar has been home base for three years, and this weekend the
boxes go into a van headed west to Santa Monica. It's not a small change — Santa Ana to
Santa Monica is a different city, different commute culture, different cost of living by a
factor I don't want to do math on. The reason is simple: three of the companies I'm most
interested in are west-side shops, and an hour-and-forty commute each way was starting
to price itself out of the equation before I even had an offer.

The apartment is smaller by about two hundred square feet, which means the electronics
bench goes from "a dedicated room" to "a corner of the living room." The Printrbot is
coming but gets a smaller footprint. The oscilloscope stays in storage until there's a
reason to drag it out. I've done more meaningful hardware work on a dining table than I
have on a dedicated bench anyway, so I'm not catastrophizing about the square footage.

The real change is the commute: eight minutes to the Spark Networks office if the offer
comes through, which is a number I don't think I'll ever stop appreciating after three
years of Southern California freeway arithmetic. Fingers crossed the interview process
reaches a conclusion quickly, because I'd like to know which direction I'm going before
the boxes are unpacked.
