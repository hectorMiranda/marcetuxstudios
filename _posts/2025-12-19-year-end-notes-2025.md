The year in one sentence: I got a studio, shipped more hardware projects than any year
since 2013, and the AI agent discipline matured from "interesting experiment" to
"repeatable engineering." Not a bad sentence.

The consulting work is the steadiest it's been. The MCP and agent architecture work
turned into a recognizable service — I can describe what I do and why it matters without
the conversation becoming a technology explanation. Clients come with a specific problem
(their RAG quality is poor, their agent is unreliable, their LLM costs are unclear)
and I have a repeatable engagement structure for each of them. That's not exciting to
write about but it's the sign that the work has matured past the exploration phase.

Looking at 2026: there's an opportunity I'm not quite ready to write about yet. Something
that would change the daily work structure. The studio will stay — the address is too
good and the setup is too useful to walk away from. The bench, the rack, the projects.
That part of the plan doesn't change. What might change is how I fill the working hours.
More on that once there's something concrete to say.
