December was the month the pipeline ran in production under real load and held. Holiday
peak came and went — queue depth spiked, Auto Scaling responded, DLQ caught the one
template bug, Spot instances kept the cost from scaling with the traffic as fast as it
could have. The engineering work from the fall earned its keep in one week of December,
which is the only real validation that matters.

Swift going open source was the event I'd been waiting for before taking iOS development
seriously as a personal investment. The Linux port and the public evolution process are
the signals that this isn't a platform-lock-in play that reverses in five years.
Reading through the Swift stdlib source on GitHub over the holiday is the kind of thing
I do for fun, which probably says something about me.

React controlled inputs closed out the year's UI work. The year started with Angular 1.3
at Spark and ended with React controlled forms and PropTypes at JibJab — not the
trajectory I planned in January but exactly the right one in retrospect. The component
model travels. ES2015 is standard. Let's Encrypt is real. Good year to have paid
attention.
