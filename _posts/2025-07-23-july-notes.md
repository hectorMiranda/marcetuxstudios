Hot July in Lincoln Heights. The studio gets afternoon sun on the west wall and I've
started using the ceiling fan I installed in March more than I expected to. The thermal
monitoring from the CO2 boards is useful — I can see the studio temperature correlate
exactly with the GPU workload on the workstation during long compile or inference runs.
The UPS battery is also showing its age under that load; something to budget for.

The RF board is up and feeding the spectrum monitor. Three years of "I'll finish that
hardware someday" resolved in a month once the bench was set up properly. The software
side of the spectrum monitor is the next project but I'm in no hurry — the system is
already collecting data and the analysis can come incrementally.

The consulting work is steady. The fine-tuning explanation post is one I've been wanting
to write for a while because I've had that conversation on six different engagements and
the answer is always the same. Prompt versioning is another one where the right pattern
is simple and under-adopted. The posts that come out of repeated client conversations
tend to be more useful than the posts about technology I'm exploring — worth keeping
that ratio in mind.
