January was mostly about recalibrating. The last two months of 2022 were noisy —
FTX collapse, ChatGPT, the general crypto-market turbulence — and the new year's main
job was figuring out what to pay attention to. I wrote more in my notes app than
anywhere else, which usually means I'm thinking more than building, and that's fine
for January.

On the tooling front: Copilot is in the editor and staying; ChatGPT is a consulting
partner I'm learning to distrust at the right moments; and the WASM Component Model is
the thing I'm reading carefully because it looks like it might matter a lot in two
years. The Rust error-type discipline from this month has already cleaned up the
contract client code in ways that matter when you're debugging a failed deploy at
11 pm.

The blockchain skepticism I wrote down on the 19th feels honest and a little scary to
have said out loud, but it's the thing I'd have told a friend in November and suppressed
for the blog. February: back to shipping. The disillusionment doesn't expire the work
in front of me.
