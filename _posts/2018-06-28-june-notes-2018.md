Two months into CTM and the ramp-up is done. I have a working mental model of the
system, I've shipped a real improvement (the reporting tier is down to three hours),
and I understand which parts of the Mulesoft layer I should learn versus defer to
the integration specialists. The new-job anxiety has been replaced by the normal kind,
which is a good sign.

The .NET Core 2.1 upgrade is worth doing and I'm pushing for it on the greenfield
services we're starting. The LTS designation matters in an enterprise that has to
defend its upgrade cadence to a security committee. The IHttpClientFactory change
alone would have saved us from a connection exhaustion incident I've now seen at two
different companies.

The drone is flying. The arm is repaired. The capacitor is replaced. My PID tune is
somewhere between "flyable" and "actually tuned" and will stay there until I get
enough field time to distinguish pilot error from firmware parameters. The KiCad
board has been shelved through the job change — June is the month I'll restart it
with fresh eyes.
