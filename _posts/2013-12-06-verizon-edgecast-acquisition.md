The official press release dropped Wednesday: Verizon is acquiring EdgeCast. The rumor
had been circulating internally for a few weeks, so the announcement wasn't a shock,
but the reality of it is a different kind of feeling than a rumor. I've been here
building the customer portal for about two years. The engineering work has been good,
the CDN domain knowledge has been deep, and now there's a large telecom company in the
picture and I genuinely don't know what that means for the day-to-day.

Large company acquisitions of small-ish technical companies tend to follow patterns.
Sometimes the technical team gets resources and integration opportunities. Sometimes
the technical team gets policies and process. I've read enough acquisition postmortems
to know that the press release language about "preserving culture" and "empowering the
team" is not a good predictor. The better predictor is how the acquiring company
actually treats the engineering staff in the first six months.

My plan is the same one I'd have in any uncertain situation: keep the work quality high,
keep learning the domain, and don't make assumptions about what the organizational
structure looks like in six months. The stack is solid, the tests are in place, the
documentation is better than it's ever been. Those are the things I control. The
integration plan between Verizon and EdgeCast's CDN infrastructure is not something I
control, and worrying about it competes with doing the work well.
