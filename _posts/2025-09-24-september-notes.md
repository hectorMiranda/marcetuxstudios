September brought real fall weather to Lincoln Heights — cooler evenings, and the west
wall finally stopped being the solar oven it was in July and August. The studio is more
comfortable to work in late now than it was all summer. The thermal monitoring data has
a clear inflection point around September 10th in the temperature chart, which is a
more precise measurement of "summer ended" than I've ever had before.

The power monitor board schematic is done and routing starts this week. The security
for agentic systems post was overdue — I've been meaning to write it for two months and
kept finding reasons to defer. It's the post I send to clients before a security review
now, which is a useful signal that it covers the right ground.

The agent framework evaluation criteria came from a conversation with a client who'd
already committed to one framework before asking me to look at it. Polite conversation.
The questions I laid out in the post are what I wish they'd had before the commitment.
Write it down so the next team can ask before, not after.
