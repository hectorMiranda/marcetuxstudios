The first week at City National Bank made the other two new-job first weeks this year
feel casual. Banking is a regulated environment in a way that software-startup and
corporate-travel weren't: every tool requires approval, every environment change
goes through a change management ticket, and the onboarding checklist alone takes
three days. None of this is bureaucracy for its own sake — most of it is traceable to
an audit requirement or a past incident — but it's a different operating rhythm than
anything I've worked in before.

The architecture team is six people, which is larger than any architecture function
I've been part of. The work is split between active project delivery — I'm assigned to
the API modernization program — and governance, which means reviewing proposals from
application teams and enforcing the standards the architecture team publishes. The
governance piece is new to me. In every previous role I was an individual
contributor who might push back on a design; here I'm part of the group whose
opinion counts toward whether a design gets approved.

The technical landscape is what you'd expect from a bank with a long history: a mix
of mainframe integration, Java middleware, and a set of more recent .NET and Node
services. Azure is the approved cloud platform. The API modernization program is about
exposing capabilities through a consistent API layer rather than direct system
integration. This is where I'll spend most of my time.
