October was the month of patterns named and unnamed. The outbox pattern finally
has a write-up I can point teammates at — two engineers have asked about reliable
event publishing this year and I've been answering verbally. The `async void` post
is a bug I found, diagnosed, and wrote down so the next person doesn't spend a day
on it. The .NET 5 migration guide is already being used: two services migrated
this week by colleagues who followed it without asking me a single question. That's
the test.

The side project shipping feels meaningful even though it's small. The Tailwind
lessons are real and I'll carry them to the next internal front-end project. The
design-system-as-Tailwind-components approach is something I want to propose for the
bank's internal tooling.

November: .NET 5 ships. Apple Silicon M1 is supposed to ship in November too, which
is a genuinely interesting moment — ARM-native macOS performance claims are
outrageous in benchmarks. I'll write about what .NET 5 actually changes from the
GA release rather than another preview note. Six months of previews deserve a proper
retrospective.
