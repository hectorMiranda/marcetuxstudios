May felt like a month where the earlier work paid off. The SignalR backplane means the
second web server can serve clients without them noticing; promises untangled the
callback nesting that had been accumulating in the Angular service layer; the directive
pattern cleaned up the chart widget duplication I'd been meaning to address since
January. Architecture-as-debt: it wasn't blocking anything until it was.

The Vagrant/Ansible swap was the maintenance work I keep procrastinating and then don't
regret doing — idempotent provisioning is one of those properties that makes a whole
class of debugging impossible because the problem can't compound over multiple runs.
Parameterized queries everywhere should have been a policy from day one; better late
than a four-second query every few requests.

V1.1 boards are on their way. This batch I actually understand what I'm waiting to
find out — the footprint check was thorough, the continuity check caught a net mismatch
before it went to fab, and I feel cautiously good about it. June: the board should
arrive, the reporting surface should be live, and I want to look at the Web API 2
attribute routing for a greenfield endpoint rather than a proof-of-concept.
