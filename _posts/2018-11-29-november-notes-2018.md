November felt like the month where the City National Bank role started to feel like
mine rather than borrowed. The OWASP review was the first time I ran a security
architecture conversation rather than participated in one, and the BOLA finding was
satisfying in the specific way that finding a real vulnerability is satisfying — not
because it exists, but because the fix is clear and the control is being built into
the policy layer rather than documented in a wiki no one will read.

The idempotency work is in code review and should ship in December. ARM templates for
the shared infrastructure are merged and the manual portal configuration is deprecated.
The discipline is real now rather than planned.

On the bench: the pulse-oximeter board is working, which closes a thread that started
in January. The drone hasn't flown since the August repairs. The KiCad migration from
Eagle is complete enough that I wouldn't go back — every new design starts in KiCad
and the 3D viewer alone is worth the switch. December is likely light on hardware
time; the bank has a deployment freeze that will push some feature work into January
and I'd rather use the quiet to actually think rather than sprint before it ends.
