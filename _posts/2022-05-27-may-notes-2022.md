May was a month of depth over breadth. The contract security work was sobering in a
useful way — reading post-mortems of EVM exploits and mapping the patterns to the Casper
execution model is time well spent even when the attack surface turns out to be smaller.
Understanding where the risk lives is different from assuming it doesn't exist, and the
discipline of writing the state update before the external call is one of those rules
that you follow even on the days it feels like ceremony.

The Rust async work has me building the indexer tooling with more confidence. Tokio's
model is the right one for this kind of I/O-heavy fan-out — talking to a dozen nodes,
collecting events, writing to storage — and the composability of futures lets me
structure the concurrency in a way that's legible in the source code rather than
hidden behind threads I'm not sure I'm using right.

The KiCad board came back from the fab this week: all joints good, all footprints
correct. A rare clean run. The SMT assembly on the sensor board is the thing that made
the difference — fewer hand-soldered joints means fewer cold solder mysteries in the
field. Next month: the Rust async indexer actually shipping, and the annual hackathon
planning is starting.
