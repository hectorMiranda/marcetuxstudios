I've been looking at commercial spaces in the northeast LA area — Lincoln Heights,
Boyle Heights, El Sereno. Not aggressively, not with a timeline I'd commit to, but
seriously enough to have walked through a few. The idea that was a note in March and
a direction in June is now something I check listings for on weekday mornings before
the consulting day starts. That's a different relationship with an idea.

What I'm looking for is specific enough to be useful as a constraint: 400–600 square
feet, ground floor for equipment moving in and out, some natural light, enough power
for a workbench setup, reasonable internet access. Not a fancy office. A working
space. The distinction matters because I've noticed that a lot of the commercial
listings in the price range I can afford are former retail spaces that have been
sitting empty since 2020 and are being listed as "creative studio" without any of
the amenities that phrase implies. Walking through tells you things photos don't.

The honest version of the plan is this: the consulting work is sustainable, the
tutoring is growing by referral, and together they cover the costs I have now plus
some margin. A dedicated space would add rent to the costs. The math works if I add
one more consulting client. That's achievable. What I'm actually waiting for is the
right space at the right price, and that's just a search problem. I'm working on it.
