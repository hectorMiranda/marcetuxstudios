The workbench is assembled and the first evening I spent in the space with the
soldering iron out and Ollama running on the Pi rack felt exactly like I'd hoped it
would. Nothing remarkable happened — I was routing a KiCad board for a sensor project
that had been sitting in a drawer for two months — but I finished the routing in two
hours instead of starting it three different times on the kitchen table and stopping.
The environment creates the session.

The setup so far: a full-length workbench along the north wall with the oscilloscope
and power supply on the left side, soldering station in the center, laptop stand and
keyboard on the right. The Pi cluster is on a small wall-mount rack above the bench.
The machines are on a folding table I'll replace with something better, but everything
is running: k3s cluster healthy, Ollama serving models, the MCP server I wrote last
week loading notes from the local filesystem. It's minimal and functional, which is
how I prefer to start.

The space needs: a better chair, a cable management pass on the rack, a proper shelf
for PCB supplies. The to-do list for the space is bounded and concrete, which is
refreshing after months of "the space" being a concept. Now it's a place with
specific things that need to happen in it. That's a better problem to have.
