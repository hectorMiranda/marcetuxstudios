March was the month the LLM space stopped feeling like hype and started feeling like
a shift. GPT-4 released, the RAG pattern became a phrase everybody uses, and I built
my first end-to-end retrieval pipeline that I'd actually trust to give a useful
answer. The personal notes experiment on the Pi is still running and I query it
nearly every day. That's the real adoption signal — not "I could use this" but "I
already do."

At work, the Rust deep dive continued — lifetimes clicked in a way they hadn't
before, and the error-type discipline from January is paying back in cleaner code.
The blockchain work is technically solid. The product-layer skepticism I wrote about in
January hasn't resolved, but it also hasn't gotten worse. I'm showing up and shipping.

The thing I'm sitting with: I'm learning two very different stacks simultaneously.
Rust and smart contracts on the job side; Python, embeddings, and language models
on the side. Usually when I do that one of them wins and the other becomes a footnote.
I'm not sure which one wins this time. That uncertainty is uncomfortable and
probably important.
