Four weeks of writing things down. The experiment is working: twice this month I
hit a problem, half-remembered solving it, and found the answer in my own post
instead of rediscovering it. That alone justifies the habit.

What the month clustered around, looking back:

- **The web is getting pushy and responsive.** SignalR and Bootstrap both showed
  up in the same rebuild — real-time data and layouts that reflow.
- **Structure is creeping into the front end.** Knockout and Backbone are me
  admitting jQuery soup doesn't scale past a certain page.
- **Shipping is becoming boring, on purpose.** Git, Jenkins, Octopus, Vagrant —
  the theme is making builds and environments reproducible so they stop being
  events.
- **The bench is leaking into the day job.** The Pi-and-Arduino "sense here,
  remember there" split is the same instinct as "cache the reads, keep state where
  it belongs."

I added an `examples/` folder this month so the posts aren't just talk — the TOTP
generator, the sensor logger, the covering-index demo, and a Knockout dashboard
all live there now. Next month I want to go deeper on a couple of these rather than
wider. See you in October.

— marcetux
