The gap between roles has given me something I haven't had in a while: time to set
up the bench properly. I've always worked from the kitchen table or a corner of a
bedroom. Tools migrate between boxes. PCB work competes with dinner. The soldering
iron lives in a drawer. It works, technically, but there's a friction to starting
that accumulates into sessions that don't happen.

I've been thinking about what a real space would look like. Not just a corner — a
room, or better, a separate space, where the oscilloscope and the soldering station
stay set up. Where the Pi cluster has its own shelf and the ethernet run doesn't
loop around a door. The kind of place where you walk in and the environment says
*this is where the work happens*. I've started calling this idea "marcetuxstudios"
when I think about it, which is probably premature naming for something that's still
a daydream.

The consulting work is covering bills. The tutoring is grounding me. But the bench
project — running a quantized model locally while simultaneously debugging a KiCad
footprint — is where the thinking happens. I want to stop doing it on improvised
surfaces. This is not an announcement; it's just writing down a direction.
