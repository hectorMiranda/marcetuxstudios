OpenAI launched ChatGPT tonight. I've been playing with it for the past two hours and
I need to write down what I'm thinking because I'm not sure I'll have this reaction
again — the first time you use something that changes the shape of the landscape.

The demos I'd seen of GPT-3 and GPT-3.5 through the API were impressive in specific
contexts and frustrating in others. Confident wrong answers, factual hallucinations,
a sense of fluency masking lack of grounding. ChatGPT in the RLHF-tuned form that
shipped tonight is a materially different experience. It follows instructions, it admits
uncertainty, it stays on task across a multi-turn conversation in a way that feels
qualitatively different. I asked it to explain a subtle point in the Casper storage
model, then pushed back on part of the answer, then asked for a code example. It handled
all three turns coherently. I didn't expect that.

I've been watching ML progress for years — the image generation arc was gradual enough
that I could track it step by step. This feels like a discontinuity. I don't know what
it means for software engineering, for documentation, for how I'd onboard a junior
engineer onto the Rust contract codebase. I'm not writing any of that speculative essay
tonight because I don't trust my judgment three hours after first contact with a new
thing. What I'll say is: I'm going to use this extensively, pay attention to where it
helps and where it misleads, and write the considered post in a few weeks when I
actually know something.
