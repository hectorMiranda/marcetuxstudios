August was a heads-down technical month — no travel, no hackathons, mostly the multi-sig
work and the type system posts. The contract we're building is getting to the size where
design decisions from month one are coming back to bite or reward depending on how
carefully they were made. The dictionary-vs-named-key lesson from April paid off in
the multi-sig implementation; the initial prototype I threw together would have been
linear in the number of signers.

The newtype post came out of actual refactoring pain, which is the best kind of post
to write. You know you have something worth writing about when fixing the bug makes you
feel genuinely embarrassed about the code that was there before, and the embarrassment
has a specific structure — "I knew the units were different, I just didn't say so in
the types." The type system is asking you to be explicit. Be explicit.

Stable Diffusion keeps getting better. I generated the concept art for the next revision
of the energy monitoring board using an SD model fine-tuned on technical illustrations.
It's not replacing a real PCB layout — nothing is doing that yet — but as a way of
rapidly sketching "does this form factor look right" before committing to a layout, it's
more useful than I expected.
