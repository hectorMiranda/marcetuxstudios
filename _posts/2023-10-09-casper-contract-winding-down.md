The role at CasperLabs ends this month. That's confirmed now, not a rumor. The
company is restructuring around what the token economics can support, and the
engineering headcount doesn't fit. I'm not surprised and I'm not especially upset —
I saw enough in August to start landing softly, and the portfolio of evening projects
turned out to be better preparation than I'd have done if the role felt stable.

The work I'm proudest of from the last 14 months: the smart contract auditing
methodology we developed, the async gRPC event streaming client, and a deploy tooling
library that reduced the "submit deploy and wait for finality" boilerplate to a
well-tested, documented module. That last one is open source and I'll maintain it even
after I leave, because I spent too much time getting it right to let it go stale.

The Rust I learned here is the kind that sticks. Not "I know enough to answer
interview questions" but "I've been paged at odd hours because a contract reverted and
had to debug it in production" Rust. That's different, and it's the part I'll carry
forward. The blockchain industry is going through its reckoning; the language and the
discipline it produces are entirely portable.
