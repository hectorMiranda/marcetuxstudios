Bitcoin has been above $15,000 this week and the conversation has changed from "is this
a bubble" to "when does the bubble end," which is different mostly in that the second
question is unanswerable. I wrote about this in July and October from the same position:
interested in the technology, skeptical of the speculation, not invested in the tokens
beyond the small amount I bought as an experiment in 2013.

The part of December's price action that interests me technically: the futures market
launched on CBOE this week. Traditional financial instruments are now settled on Bitcoin
price, which pulls institutional money into a market that was previously retail-only.
That changes the volatility profile in ways that I don't think anyone can fully model in
real time. It's not straightforwardly good or bad — it brings legitimacy and liquidity
and also brings the risk management practices of traditional finance, which is a different
kind of participant than the enthusiasts who drove the price to this point.

What I'm holding: the small amount from 2013, unchanged. The position I'm in is that
any gain from here is relative to a cost basis that made the whole thing a successful
bet on a technology experiment. I'm not selling it in a panic and not buying more in a
frenzy. The technology is still interesting; the price is someone else's problem to
time.
