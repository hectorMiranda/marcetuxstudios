June split my attention in an interesting way. Half of it was deep Casper work —
the indexer is running, the storage design lessons are internalized, the async Rust
patterns are becoming automatic. The other half was the image generation tools, which
I did not expect to spend significant time on but couldn't put down once I started.

The DALL-E 2 and Stable Diffusion posts are the first time I've written on this blog
about something that isn't engineering work in the service of building a system.
Image generation isn't part of my job at CasperLabs and isn't something I'm building
with. But the rate of capability change is too fast to ignore, and writing about it
is how I process what I think about something. I don't know yet what to do with
diffusion models professionally; I know they're real and they're here.

The indexer is the part of June I'm most professionally satisfied with. Good async
architecture is the kind of work that's invisible when it's working — events flow,
writes happen, the database is current — and I think this one is going to keep working
without much intervention. That's what you want from infrastructure: boring at the
boundary.
