The rack is in, the bench is working, and the first PCB session in the new space is
done. That's the lab move-in complete in all the ways that matter. What remains is
cosmetic — the cable management on the bench left side, the monitor arm, eventually
some acoustic treatment on the wall behind the desk. The functional stuff is all
running.

On the consulting side the agent workflow patterns from January and February are
becoming standard practice on engagements. Clients are now asking specifically about
MCP rather than just "how do we wire this agent to our data," which is a sign the
protocol is getting traction faster than I expected. Guardrails and evals — the
output-side discipline — is still the gap most teams arrive at late. Planning to write
more on that.

The first KiCad project in the studio went faster than I expected and the board is at
the fab. WebGPU was a nice detour — genuinely useful for a client problem, not just
interesting. The month felt grounded in a way the previous few didn't, probably because
the space is finally what I imagined it being when I signed the papers in January.
