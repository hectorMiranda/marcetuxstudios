December split itself neatly in two: the first half was Log4Shell noise and the
engineering reading that comes with it, and the second half was genuinely quiet —
the first real stretch of unfettered reading and building I've had in years. The
Casper technical documentation is dense and serious and I've been working through
it at a pace that feels like graduate study rather than weekend reading. Good.

The Rust lifetimes post is the one I'm most satisfied with this year. It took three
weeks of incubation to write two paragraphs that I think are actually accurate.
The slow-burn understanding is the right phrase for it — the concept arrives not all
at once but in layers, each read adding a bit more structure to what was previously
noise. I've been writing this blog since 2013 partly because writing forces the
understanding to sharpen, and that post is the clearest example of it working.

The LoRa gateway board came back from fab this week. Clean boards, no silkscreen
issues, the DFM discipline is now reflexive. Soldering is scheduled for the first
week of January. 2022 starts with a soldering iron and a new employer and Rust as
the primary tool, which is an odd collection of things and feels exactly right.
The year-end is never a conclusion; it's just a convenient bookmark. The work
continues.
