Big month. Blazor WebAssembly went GA and it's as good as the previews suggested —
I have a small internal reporting tool in Blazor WASM that's been running for two
weeks and the developer experience is better than anything I've shipped in a while.
Deno 1.0 is interesting without being urgent; I'll use it for scripting where I'd
have used ts-node before. The pipeline security work — environment gates, Trivy
scanning, pinned base images — is unglamorous and permanently on the to-do list
until it's done, then immediately invisible.

Working remote at month two is still an adjustment. The incident process is running
well; the social deficit is real and not something tooling can fix. I've started
doing short one-on-ones with the team just to talk through what's hard without an
open incident channel in the background. That's not an engineering decision, but
it's the one that seems to matter most right now.

June: I want to get deeper into the Tailwind workflow on the side project, and there's
a Kubernetes upgrade on the AKS cluster that's been deferred twice. Also time to
revisit the energy dashboard — the ghost load from March is still unidentified and
that's been bothering me longer than it should.
