Slow month to write — the first weeks of January are always heavy operationally, and
this year layered a round of hiring conversations on top of the usual incident review
debt. But the work moved. The API is running on .NET Core 2.0 and the performance
numbers are worth the porting cost. We're on GKE now rather than ECS, and I'm glad
we made the switch before the services multiplied rather than after.

The GraphQL experiment is on ice — the mobile team's pain is real but the N+1 surface
and the added operational layer convinced me to solve it first with better REST
conventions and a projection parameter before introducing a whole new query language.
Sometimes the answer to a tool question is "not yet." The Angular 5 upgrade shipped
clean; the new `HttpClient` interceptor approach finally killed the base-service-class
inheritance I'd been embarrassed by for two years.

On the bench: I've been laying out a small KiCad board for a pulse-oximeter logger
for the clinic use case — nothing shipping, just a curiosity — and I got to spin up
the drone again for the first time since October. Good reset after a dense week.
