Three weeks since the Verizon/EdgeCast announcement and the day-to-day is largely
unchanged. The engineering work is the same work. The customer portal serves the same
customers. The questions from leadership are more frequent and more administrative than
usual — documentation of systems, headcount reports, process descriptions that feel
like they're for an audience outside the building. That's expected and probably healthy.

The uncertainty is real but diffuse. The acquisition hasn't been completed; this is the
announcement, not the close. The actual organizational changes, if any, come after the
close. The engineers I talk to are doing what engineers in uncertain situations do: heads
down, work high-quality, watch for signals. The signals so far are: the team stays in
place, the technical roadmap is intact as described, and nobody's asked us to change
anything fundamental about how we work.

What I've noticed about my own reaction is that the uncertainty bothers me less than I
would have expected. The work is interesting, the CDN domain is deep enough that the
knowledge is genuinely durable regardless of where I end up next, and the stack I've
built this year is something I understand well enough to rebuild or adapt elsewhere.
Skills don't get acquired. The org structure that holds them might.
