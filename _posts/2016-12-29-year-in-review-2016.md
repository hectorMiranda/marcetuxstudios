Looking back at the year's posts, the through-line is reliability at the boundary:
dead-letter queues, idempotent workers, circuit breakers, immutable deployments,
structured monitoring. The pipeline at JibJab is more reliable at exit than it was at
entry, and the reliability improvements are mostly patterns rather than heroics —
standard tools applied deliberately. The heroics come from not having applied the
patterns.

On the language and framework side: TypeScript 2.0 and Angular 2 final are the
combination I'll use for new front-end projects. .NET Core 1.0 is the platform for
new backend work. KiCad is my PCB tool now; Eagle is gone from the bench. The ESP32
is what the ESP8266 should have been, and the first board is at a fab in Shenzhen.

The career move is the big event. JibJab was a good two years; the media platform
problems are worth having solved. SolidCommerce and marketplace APIs are next. The
pattern from 2013 forward has been one domain per job — CDN, then dating, then media,
now retail/marketplace — and each domain leaves something behind that the next one
builds on. The pipeline thinking from JibJab will show up in the marketplace work;
I'm confident of that even without knowing the specifics yet. The goal for 2017:
learn the domain faster than I did each previous time.
