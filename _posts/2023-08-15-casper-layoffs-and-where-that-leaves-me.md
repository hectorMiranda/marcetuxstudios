CasperLabs announced a round of layoffs last week. I'm not in this cut, but the
writing is clear enough that I'm not going to pretend I don't see it. Crypto winter
is doing what crypto winters do, and a company built on a specific blockchain needs
that blockchain to be valued highly enough to fund operations. The token price does not
cooperate with that requirement right now.

This is the part of a job where you have two choices: update the resume quietly and
start looking, or wait and see. I'm doing both. The technical work here has been
genuinely good — a year of serious Rust, real distributed-systems problems, smart
contract security, async networking. That doesn't evaporate with the company. The
resume is stronger for this year even if the role ends sooner than planned.

The direction I'm pointing is not toward another blockchain role. I wrote in January
about watching the LLM space with interest; I've spent the last seven months building
in that space on weekends and evenings. The next job I'm looking for involves LLMs,
retrieval systems, and the product engineering around them. Blockchain was a real
education. The next one is the one I want.
