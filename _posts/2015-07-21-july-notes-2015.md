July was two distinct weeks in two distinct jobs. Last week at Spark was wrapping up
loose ends and doing proper knowledge transfer — documenting the Couchbase view schemas,
walking the team through the SQS worker configuration, leaving things findable. The
first two weeks at JibJab were the opposite: ingesting as fast as possible.

Ember Data's identity map clicked partway through the second week, which was the
moment the framework stopped feeling like ceremony and started feeling like a tool with
a point of view. S3 lifecycle policies are genuinely interesting once you see the bill
they're optimizing — it's applied economics as much as infrastructure work.

The FFMPEG homework I did in June is paying off: I can read the team's pipeline code
and understand what the filter graphs are doing rather than treating them as opaque
command lines. There's a lot more to learn — the face-swap compositing pipeline uses
FFMPEG features I've only read about — but having the vocabulary from day one means I'm
asking better questions than I would have been otherwise. Culver City, new job, new
stack. Summer is off to a good start.
