The ESP8266 started appearing in forum posts in July and by now the maker community is
paying attention. It's a $4 WiFi module with a built-in TCP/IP stack and a 32-bit
processor. The module itself is programmable via AT commands over serial, and some
people are already flashing custom firmware directly onto the ESP8266 chip.

The math on IoT just changed. Before this, adding WiFi to a project meant either an
Arduino with a WiFi shield (bulky and expensive) or a Raspberry Pi (power hungry and
overkill for a simple sensor). The ESP8266 can read a sensor and POST the reading to a
server over HTTPS for less power than a Pi takes just to boot. For the kind of
room-temperature and door-state sensors I've been thinking about for the apartment, this
is the right tool.

My first experiments are serial: an Arduino Uno sends AT commands to the ESP8266 and
reads the acknowledgments back. The command set is simple — `AT+CWJAP` to join an
access point, `AT+CIPSTART` to open a TCP connection, `AT+CIPSEND` to write bytes. It's
verbose but it works and the latency from command to acknowledged TCP connection is
about two seconds. The community is already working on nicer abstractions. Paying
attention to this one.
