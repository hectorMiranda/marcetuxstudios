I am writing this a week after handing in my notice at Go RN, which gives me enough
distance to be honest about it without being either defensive or sentimental. The
company is not shutting down — I'm leaving for a role that makes sense for where I
am in my career, and the transition is planned and professional. But any time you
leave something you built, it's worth sitting with why.

What I did well: hired people I trusted, made architectural decisions we could grow
into rather than ones that required rewriting the year after, and kept the API surface
consistent enough that the mobile team had a stable contract to build against. The
Kubernetes migration, the .NET Core bet — both held up. What I'd do differently: write
things down sooner. The system diagram I drew for the new hire's first day should have
existed in January 2017, not March 2018.

The new role is at Corporate Travel Management, which is a different problem set —
larger team, more process, corporate integrations and B2B workflows rather than the
patient-scheduling domain. I'm taking the senior backend architect title rather than
CTO, which is an honest reflection of what I want to do: less hiring and vendor
management, more design and code. Sometimes the step back in responsibility is the
step toward the work you actually want to do.
