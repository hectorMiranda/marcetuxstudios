After about a year of building it out, the marcetuxstudios space in Lincoln Heights is operational. The bench is in, the home lab rack is on the wall, the Altium workstation actually has room to breathe, and I ran Ethernet drops to the spots I need them. It's not large — three hundred square feet with a dedicated ventilation circuit for soldering and an under-bench UPS that covers the lab gear during the grid hiccups this neighborhood has in July — but it's *mine*, and it's organized in a way I designed, and that changes how I work.

Having a space separate from the apartment means the project is always mid-state, not packed away to make room for dinner. The bench logger I was prototyping on evenings at the kitchen table is back on the bench where I left it, probes clipped in, oscilloscope trace paused from last Tuesday. The home lab runs three nodes — a repurposed ThinkStation as a compute host plus two mini-PC nodes — with k3s handling orchestration. It's where I run my local observability stack and the services that would be embarrassing to host publicly.

I'll post about the specific setup details as I actually finish them. What I can say now is that having the right environment for this kind of work — the physical analog to having a good development machine — compounds over time. The space is already making me more consistent. The new year, the new job, and the new space all landed in the same month. I'll take it.
